Our Class

Our fitness experts can help you discover new training techniques and exercises that offer a dynamic and efficient full-body workout.

Chest

The chest includes some of the largest muscles in the upper body and you use the chest muscles all day long.In fact, when you work your chest, your shoulders and arms are also involved, allowing you to exercise more of your body at once.

Back

Adding a back-focused workout to your fitness routine will not only improve your overall strength but can reduce back pain and support great form and posture. By strengthening your back muscles, you're building up the main support structure for your entire body.

Biceps/Triceps

The two main muscles in your upper arm are the biceps in the front and the triceps in the back. They're opposite functioning muscle groups, so they require different types of exercises for strength training.

Shoulder

Having well-developed shoulders can indicate strength and health since you'll have lots of upper body muscle mass. It's recommended that you support shoulder strength with a strong back and arms as well as a lean waist. Opening your chest and drawing your shoulders back down your spine can help improve your posture.

Leg

Leg workouts engage the major muscle groups of your body, which helps to improve overall athletic performance and support healthy movement patterns in your daily life. A strong lower body will also help to prevent injury and manage chronic conditions such as arthritis, heart disease, and diabetes.

Abs

Abs workout target these back muscles and the spine, making them more flexible and less rigid, thus reducing lower back pain. These workouts also improve body posture when walking, standing or sitting, thus reducing the occurrence of back pain associated with poor posture.
